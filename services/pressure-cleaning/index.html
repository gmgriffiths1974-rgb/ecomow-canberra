How we do it

Clean surfaces, fast and without the mess.

Outdoor surfaces — driveways, paths, patios, decks, retaining walls — get grimey. Dirt, moss, algae, and mould build up, especially on south-facing areas where reduced sun and winter moisture create the perfect conditions for growth. High-pressure water cleaning makes them look new again.

Kai uses a battery-powered pressure washer that delivers the power you need without the petrol fumes or excessive noise. High-pressure water alone handles most jobs — dirt, moss, and algae come off with the pressure. Where stubborn stains need extra help, we use biodegradable cleaning solutions. Nothing harsh, nothing that harms soil, plants, or waterways.

We clean driveways, paths, concrete, pavers, decks, fences, retaining walls, and outdoor furniture. We assess each surface first to ensure we use the right pressure and technique — pavers, concrete, and decking all have different tolerances. We manage debris and runoff properly so your property stays clean and tidy during the work.

Canberra's climate means south-facing surfaces need cleaning more often than sun-exposed areas. Kai can advise on timing and frequency based on your property's aspect and shading.

FAQs

Common questions about pressure cleaning.

Can you pressure clean my driveway and paths?

Yes, that's one of our core services. We pressure clean driveways, paths, patios, concrete, pavers, and decks. High-pressure water removes dirt, moss, algae, mould, and stains. We assess the surface first to ensure the pressure and technique won't cause damage.

Will pressure cleaning damage my pavers?

Not if it's done properly. We adjust pressure and technique based on the surface — pavers, concrete, and decking all have different tolerances. We've cleaned thousands of square metres across Canberra without causing damage. We assess your surfaces first and let you know what we'd recommend.

How often should outdoor surfaces be pressure cleaned in Canberra?

South-facing surfaces that don't get much sun develop moss and algae more quickly — every 6–12 months is reasonable. North-facing areas that dry out faster might only need cleaning every 18–24 months. High-traffic areas stay cleaner. It depends on your site's aspect, shade, and how much you care about appearance. Kai can advise on your property.

Do you use chemicals when pressure cleaning?

Pressure cleaning with water alone handles most jobs — dirt, moss, and algae come off with the water pressure. Where stubborn stains need treatment, we use biodegradable cleaning solutions. We never use harsh chemicals or anything that harms soil, plants, or waterways. Battery-powered pressure washer means no petrol fumes or excessive noise either.
