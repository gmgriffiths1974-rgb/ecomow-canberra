How we do it

Clean gutters. Water flowing. Problem solved.

Canberra gutters face a specific challenge: eucalyptus trees drop leaves constantly, and autumn brings heavy falls. Blocked gutters overflow into wall cavities, cause water damage, and are a serious bushfire risk if they fill with dry debris. Clearing them regularly isn't optional — it's essential maintenance.

Kai clears gutters of all leaves, twigs, and built-up silt. Then downpipes are flushed to ensure water flows freely all the way to the ground. We check for blockages deeper in the system and let you know if there are any issues. Using a battery-powered blower makes the cleanup faster and cleaner than petrol equipment — no fumes, significantly quieter, and less noise pollution on your street.

All debris that falls during the process is collected and removed. You get clean gutters, flowing downpipes, and a property that's ready for the next rain or fire season.

FAQs

Common questions about gutter cleaning in Canberra.

How often should gutters be cleaned in Canberra?

At least twice a year — autumn after leaf drop (March-May) and spring after wind events (September-November). In Canberra, eucalyptus leaf fall is constant, so gutters can fill up quickly. If you've got trees right over your roof, quarterly cleaning prevents overflow and blockages. Check your gutters after storms as well — branches and debris can accumulate fast.

Do you clean downpipes as well?

Yes. Gutter cleaning always includes downpipe flushing. We check that water flows freely through the whole system — from gutter to ground — so no blockages cause water damage or overflow into wall cavities. If we find a blockage deeper in the downpipe, we can usually clear it or advise on what's needed.

Can you install gutter guard?

We don't install gutter guard as part of our standard service, but we can recommend reliable installers if you're interested. Gutter guard is worth considering if you've got heavy leaf fall — it reduces cleaning frequency. Good quality guard makes a real difference in Canberra where eucalyptus debris is constant. We can discuss whether it's a good fit for your situation.

Is gutter cleaning important for bushfire safety?

Yes. Clogged gutters filled with dry leaves and debris are a serious bushfire risk. Clean gutters mean water can flow freely if a fire approaches, and there's no accumulated dry material right at your roofline. Clearing gutters regularly is part of any sensible bushfire preparation plan in Canberra. We recommend a clean before fire season in spring.
